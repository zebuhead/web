The Pale Blue Dot

Bio

John holds a Bachelor of Science in Applied Environmental and Spatial Analysis. He is currently a first-year graduate student in the Energy and Resources Group at UC Berkeley. John is broadly interested in the transactional relationship between humans and their environment. Furthermore, he is interested in the manner which rapidly emerging technologies such as GIS and Remote Sensing will assist in understanding that relationship. John has research interests in land use, water , energy, sustainable development, environmental ethics, environmental history, economics, and policy. His academic pursuits span the physical and social sciences as he believes future challenges will require modes of inquiry that cross traditional disciplinary boundaries.

“Look again at that dot. That's here. That's home. That's us. On it everyone you love, everyone you know, everyone you ever heard of, every human being who ever was, lived out their lives. The aggregate of our joy and suffering, thousands of confident religions, ideologies, and economic doctrines, every hunter and forager, every hero and coward, every creator and destroyer of civilization, every king and peasant, every young couple in love, every mother and father, hopeful child, inventor and explorer, every teacher of morals, every corrupt politician, every "superstar," every "supreme leader," every saint and sinner in the history of our species lived there-on a mote of dust suspended in a sunbeam. The Earth is a very small stage in a vast cosmic arena. Think of the endless cruelties visited by the inhabitants of one corner of this pixel on the scarcely distinguishable inhabitants of some other corner, how frequent their misunderstandings, how eager they are to kill one another, how fervent their hatreds.
